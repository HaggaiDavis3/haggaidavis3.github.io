Doctoral Dissertation

Download PDF

Dr. Davis's doctoral dissertation, completed at NYU, advancing methods for understanding urban freight patterns and informing the electrification of medium and heavy-duty vehicles in New York City. Designed and implemented a mathematical optimization model to mirror real world freight truck behavior. Led the team which estimated freight demand from publicly available data. Coordinated with NYC DOT to ensure simulated truck behavior compliance. Validated model against real world data to create a synthetic freight population. Performed a market scan to determine realistic BET operations and constraints then applied those at the tour level to determine feasibility and impacts on energy consumption and GHG emissions. After quantifying limitations of a standard recharging system, Designed and implemented a mathematical optimization model to manage the trade-offs from implementing a BET swapping station system across the city in terms of battery weight (engineering complexity), station density (infrastructure cost), and fleet uptime (capability).
