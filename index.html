About

Dr. Haggai Delane Davis, III is a transportation scientist based in New York City with 10 years of experience in transportation research, planning, and engineering. He holds a PhD in Transportation Systems from New York University, an MS in Civil Engineering from Louisiana State University, and a BS in Physics and Astronomy, also from LSU. That foundation in physics and engineering shapes how he works: he is quantitative at his core, and he derives deep satisfaction from deploying analytical tools — statistical methods, machine learning, optimization, and game theory — to sort through trade-offs and surface better outcomes.

His award-winning academic career focused on urban freight and the electrification of medium- and heavy-duty vehicles in New York City, culminating in a dissertation on battery-swapping feasibility for commercial delivery fleets. His work has been recognized with the USDOT Eisenhower Graduate Fellowship, the ITS-NY Research Communication Award, the ITE MET Section Dr. Pignataro Memorial Award, and the TRB Urban Freight Paper of the Year, and has been published in Transportation Research Part A and presented at TRB, the Conference on Sustainable Mobility, and elsewhere. In his consulting practice at Arcadis, he currently leads metric design and analysis for NYC DOT's Off-Hours Delivery incentive program, the Queens Boulevard Phase 2 benefit-cost analysis, a zero-emission vehicle deployment strategy in Mexico City for the World Business Council for Sustainable Development, and ongoing connected and autonomous vehicle advisory work for the Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development.

Beyond client work and research, Haggai and his father co-host a weekly radio program and podcast on Baton Rouge's Talk 107.3 FM that explores how technological change reshapes business and society, Tech Gumbo. He is also training for the 2026 New York City Marathon.
