About

Dr. Davis is a transportation analyst with 10 years of experience in transportation research, planning, and engineering. His strong foundation in physics and engineering has prepared him to make decisions driven by relentless quantitative reasoning.

In his award-winning academic career, he led the development of new techniques for understanding urban freight patterns to advance the electrification of medium and heavy-duty vehicles in New York City. His work employed rigorous mathematical analysis to describe previously difficult-to-estimate quantities such as GHG emissions, VMT, and viability of battery electric trucks (BETs).

He has leveraged a variety of software tools for these analyses including Python, LLMs, and industry-specific software. Additionally, he cohosts weekly discussions about the impact of technological change on business and society on his radio program, Tech Gumbo.

Euchre Strategy Master

Play Live GitHub

A browser-based Euchre game with a built-in AI opponent and a post-hand move grader that evaluates every bid and card play against optimal strategy. Built with Python (FastAPI) on the backend and vanilla HTML/CSS/JS on the frontend, deployed on Railway.

  • Full Euchre rules engine including left bower, going alone, and stick-the-dealer
  • AI opponent using hand-strength scoring, trump void detection, and positional heuristics
  • Counterfactual simulation: replays each decision with the AI's choice to show what would have happened
  • Contextual narrative grade report generated after every hand

Iterative Balancing for Entropy Maximization

GitHub

A research implementation developed as part of doctoral work at NYU, referenced in Davis et al. (2023). Solves doubly constrained gravity model problems — a core challenge in transportation demand modeling — by iteratively adjusting a matrix's rows and columns to satisfy both marginal constraints while maximizing entropy.

  • Implements the iterative balancing (Fratar-inspired) algorithm from first principles
  • Applied to estimating urban freight trip distributions and spatial interaction patterns
  • Supports GHG emissions modeling and battery electric truck (BET) viability analysis for NYC
